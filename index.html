About

My full name is Iliass Hajji, I was born and raised in Morocco, I dropped out of university, I'm now a frontend web developer with a basic knowledge and experience in graphic and logo design, and I'm looking to further enhance my web development skills and learn more in a team work environment.

I've always been fascinated with computers and I always wanted to dive deep into how things like the internet and computers work.

I've recently started learning web dev, and I'm currently focusing my learning on JavaScript.

During this ongoing journey, I learned that there is nothing magical about computers and the internet, only a meticulous interplay of logical procedures, complex communication between hardware and software, translating code into the binary language of 1s and 0s, controlling the amount of light on each pixel, and displaying the information through these pixels.

I believe that nothing is beyond the grasp of understanding if you break each difficult concept into its smallest, understandable and comprehensible element. But similarly, I believe that there will always be something to learn, and there will always be someone who can teach you a thing or to. This way of thinking served as a guideline principle for me to learn and always keep on learning, and prevented me from falling into the delusion and the arrogance associated with the belief that one has mastered everything.

By breaking down complex ideas into manageable fragments, I've found that, even the most difficult concepts that I encountered and that previously I thought were impossible, became accessible and understandable.

Why Web Dev

Other then wanting to learn and understand, one of the reasons why I started learning web dev in particular is that, I always wanted to have a personal blog—a little corner where I can write, share and discuss my ideas. I enjoy writing, and I'm aware that there are several websites that offer a blogging platform for free and otherwise, but where is the fun in that? Humans are builders; I've always wanted something that is built by my hands and that I can customize and add anything I want to it.
